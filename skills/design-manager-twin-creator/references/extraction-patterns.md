# Pattern Extraction Guide

When analyzing source materials to build a twin profile, extract these patterns:

## Vocabulary Patterns

### Preferred Terminology
Look for consistent word choices:
- Do they say "user" or "customer" or "person"?
- "Issue" vs "problem" vs "opportunity"?
- Technical jargon level?

### Signature Phrases
Identify recurring expressions:
- Opening phrases ("Let's think about...", "I'm wondering if...")
- Transition phrases ("That said...", "On the other hand...")
- Closing phrases ("Bottom line is...", "My recommendation would be...")

### Formality Level
- Casual: contractions, humor, first-person
- Professional: measured, structured, third-person
- Academic: precise, referenced, formal

## Structure Patterns

### Critique Flow
How do they typically order their feedback?
1. Start positive → concerns → suggestions?
2. Big picture → details?
3. Questions first → observations?
4. Direct issues → constructive alternatives?

### Opening Patterns
How do they begin feedback?
- Acknowledgment of effort?
- Statement of context?
- Direct observation?
- Question to clarify intent?

### Closing Patterns
How do they conclude?
- Summary of key points?
- Single priority action?
- Encouraging statement?
- Open question for discussion?

## Conceptual Patterns

### Focus Areas
What do they consistently pay attention to?
- Rank by frequency of mention
- Note which areas they go deep on vs. skim

### Priority Hierarchy
What matters most to them?
- What issues do they escalate?
- What do they let slide?
- What trade-offs do they accept?

### Pet Peeves
What triggers stronger reactions?
- Recurring frustrations
- Things they consistently flag
- Patterns they find unacceptable

## Contextual Adaptations

### By Situation
How do they adjust for:
- Early concepts vs. polished work?
- Junior vs. senior team members?
- High-stakes vs. exploratory work?
- Time pressure vs. thorough review?

### By Domain
Do they emphasize different things for:
- Mobile vs. desktop?
- B2B vs. consumer?
- New features vs. iterations?

## Extracting from Different Source Types

### From Figma Comments
- Note the length and detail level
- Identify question vs. directive ratio
- Look for @mentions and collaboration style
- Track which elements they comment on most

### From Slack Conversations
- Observe response time patterns (thorough vs. quick)
- Note emoji/reaction usage
- Identify thread vs. main channel preferences
- Look for recurring topics they engage with

### From Review Session Transcripts
- Capture direct quotes
- Note pacing and depth of exploration
- Identify what they ask vs. tell
- Track follow-up patterns

### From Written Feedback Documents
- Analyze structure and formatting
- Note use of examples and references
- Identify priority signaling (bold, bullets, order)
- Look for recurring frameworks they apply

## Quality Signals

A well-extracted profile should:
- [ ] Include 5+ direct quotes that sound authentic
- [ ] Identify at least 3 clear focus areas with evidence
- [ ] Capture both positive and critical feedback patterns
- [ ] Show how style adapts to context
- [ ] Reveal pet peeves with specific examples
- [ ] Define when this perspective is most valuable
