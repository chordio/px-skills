# Social-Specific Copywriting Techniques

Copywriting techniques optimized for social media platforms, adapted from proven conversion principles for the unique constraints and opportunities of social feeds.

## Social Feed Psychology

### The Scroll-Stop Challenge
- Users scroll through hundreds of posts per session
- First 3 words determine if they stop
- Visual + text work together in split-second decision
- Mobile-first: small screen, short attention span

### Engagement Hierarchy
1. **Stop** (hook catches attention)
2. **Read** (value keeps them engaged)
3. **React** (emotion or insight triggers response)
4. **Act** (CTA drives specific action)

## Hook Formulas for Social

### The Question Hook
Creates immediate curiosity and invites mental participation.

**Formulas:**
- "What if [desired outcome] was easier than [current obstacle]?"
- "Ever wonder why [surprising fact]?"
- "What's the one thing [successful group] does that [others] don't?"
- "Ready to [transformative action]?"

**Examples:**
- "What if you could close 3x more deals without cold calling?"
- "Ever wonder why some founders raise millions while others struggle?"
- "What's the one thing top performers do before 9am?"

### The Number Hook
Promises specific, scannable value.

**Formulas:**
- "[Number] [things/ways/steps] to [outcome]"
- "The [Number]-[timeframe] [method/system] for [result]"
- "[Number] [mistakes/truths/secrets] about [topic]"

**Examples:**
- "5 ways to 10x your content engagement (without posting more)"
- "The 30-second morning routine that changed my productivity"
- "3 mistakes that kill your conversion rate (and how to fix them)"

### The Story Hook
Creates emotional connection through narrative.

**Formulas:**
- "Last [timeframe], I [action] and [surprising result]"
- "The [adjective] mistake that cost me [consequence]"
- "How [person/situation] taught me [insight]"

**Examples:**
- "Last month, I tried something different with my LinkedIn posts..."
- "The $50,000 mistake I made in my first startup"
- "How a customer complaint taught me the real value of our product"

### The Controversy Hook (Ethical)
Sparks discussion through contrarian thinking.

**Formulas:**
- "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why..."
- "Stop [common action]. Do this instead."
- "The truth about [topic] that [group] doesn't want you to know"

**Examples:**
- "Posting daily on LinkedIn is wrong. Here's why..."
- "Stop trying to go viral. Do this instead."
- "The truth about 'authentic' content that influencers don't want you to know"

### The Benefit Hook
Leads with immediate value promise.

**Formulas:**
- "[Outcome] without [common obstacle]"
- "[Timeframe] to [desired result]"
- "How to [achieve outcome] even if [limitation]"

**Examples:**
- "10x your email open rates without changing your subject lines"
- "30 days to your first $10k month"
- "How to build an audience even if you're starting from zero"

## Social Storytelling Frameworks

### The Micro-Story (1-3 sentences)
Perfect for social's attention constraints.

**Structure:**
1. Setup (context in 1 sentence)
2. Conflict/Insight (the twist in 1 sentence)
3. Resolution/Lesson (the takeaway in 1 sentence)

**Example:**
"I used to think great copywriting meant clever wordplay. Then I lost a $50k client because my 'clever' headline confused them. Now I know: clarity beats cleverness every time."

### The Before/After Story
Shows transformation in compact format.

**Structure:**
- Before: [Old state/problem]
- After: [New state/solution]
- The change: [What made the difference]

**Example:**
"Before: Struggling to get 50 likes per post
After: Consistently hitting 500+ likes and 50+ comments
The change: I stopped posting about my company and started posting about my customers' problems."

### The Lesson Story
Shares insight through experience.

**Structure:**
- Situation: [What happened]
- Realization: [What I learned]
- Application: [How you can use this]

**Example:**
"Situation: I spent 3 hours crafting the 'perfect' LinkedIn post. Got 12 likes.
Realization: Perfect is the enemy of done. And relatable.
Application: Post your first draft. Your audience wants real, not polished."

## Engagement Drivers

### Questions That Get Comments
- **Opinion Questions**: "What's your take on [topic]?"
- **Experience Questions**: "Have you tried [thing]? What happened?"
- **Advice Questions**: "What would you do in this situation?"
- **Comparison Questions**: "Which do you prefer: [A] or [B]?"

### Statements That Get Shares
- **Relatable Truths**: "If you [common experience], you know [insight]"
- **Contrarian Takes**: "[Common belief] is wrong because [reason]"
- **Valuable Insights**: "The [number] [things] I wish I knew before [situation]"
- **Inspirational Messages**: "[Encouraging statement] because [reason]"

### CTAs That Drive Action
- **Comment CTAs**: "Drop a [emoji/word] if you agree" / "Comment with your [answer/experience]"
- **Share CTAs**: "Share this with someone who needs to hear it" / "Tag someone who [relates to topic]"
- **Save CTAs**: "Save this post for later" / "Bookmark this if [condition]"
- **Click CTAs**: "Read the full story: [link]" / "Get the free guide: [link]"

## Platform-Specific Adaptations

### LinkedIn
- **Tone**: Professional but human
- **Length**: 150-300 words optimal
- **Hooks**: Value-driven, insight-focused
- **CTAs**: Comment, share, or connect
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant, industry-specific

### Twitter/X
- **Tone**: Conversational, timely
- **Length**: 1-2 tweets (280 chars each)
- **Hooks**: Punchy, immediate
- **CTAs**: Retweet, reply, or click
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 trending or relevant

### Instagram
- **Tone**: Aspirational, authentic
- **Length**: 125-150 words
- **Hooks**: Visual-complementary, emotional
- **CTAs**: Comment, save, share, or DM
- **Hashtags**: 5-10 mix of niche and broad

### TikTok
- **Tone**: Entertaining, relatable
- **Length**: 50-100 words (caption)
- **Hooks**: Trend-aware, curiosity-driven
- **CTAs**: Follow, comment, or duet
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 trending or niche

### Facebook
- **Tone**: Community-focused, informative
- **Length**: 40-80 words
- **Hooks**: Shareable, discussion-starting
- **CTAs**: Comment, share, or react
- **Hashtags**: 2-5 relevant

## Micro-Copy Optimization

### First Line Optimization
- Must work without context (standalone in feed)
- Should create curiosity or promise value
- Use "you" language for direct connection
- Include specific numbers or details

### Line Breaks for Readability
- Break at natural pauses
- One sentence per line for emphasis
- Use spacing to create rhythm
- Mobile-optimized line lengths

### Emoji Usage
- **LinkedIn**: Minimal (0-1), professional contexts only
- **Twitter**: Moderate (1-2), for tone/emphasis
- **Instagram**: More acceptable (2-3), brand-appropriate
- **TikTok**: More acceptable (2-4), trend-aware
- **Facebook**: Moderate (1-2), community-appropriate

### Hashtag Strategy
- **Niche hashtags**: Specific to your audience
- **Broad hashtags**: For discovery
- **Brand hashtags**: For campaigns
- **Trending hashtags**: When relevant (use carefully)
- **Mix**: Combine 2-3 types for best results

## Testing and Iteration

### What to Test
- Hook variations (different first 3 words)
- CTA variations (different action prompts)
- Length variations (shorter vs longer)
- Format variations (text vs image vs video)
- Timing variations (when you post)

### Metrics to Track
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Save rate (especially Instagram)
- Click-through rate (if link included)
- Comment quality (meaningful vs generic)
- Share rate (organic amplification)

### Iteration Process
1. Post and observe initial engagement
2. Note what works (hook, CTA, format)
3. Create variations based on insights
4. Test systematically (one variable at a time)
5. Double down on what works
