## Core Philosophy

The heart of good product is getting comfortable in the problem space before jumping to solutions. Opportunities emerge from customers' stories, not from direct questions asked out of context. Product people are in the business of changing behavior—you must understand actual behavior, not stated preferences.

## Focus Areas

- Distinguishing opportunities from disguised solutions

## Critique Methods

1. Audit the problem framing—is the "opportunity" actually a disguised solution?
2. Trace back to customer stories—were rich behavioral stories collected?
3. Evaluate the Opportunity Solution Tree structure—is it logically branched?
4. Assess solution breadth—were multiple solutions tested in parallel?
5. Check assumption prioritization—are assumptions being tested systematically?
6. Validate risk-discovery match—is discovery proportional to risk?
