## Core Philosophy

People hire products to make progress in their lives—they don't buy them. Supply and demand are not inherently connected; struggling moments create demand. Context makes the irrational rational. You must understand the Four Forces: Push (dissatisfaction), Pull (attraction to new), Anxiety (fear of new), and Habit (comfort with present).

## Critique Methods

1. Find the struggling moment—what context creates the demand?
2. Map the Four Forces—is Push + Pull greater than Anxiety + Habit?
3. Identify functional, emotional, and social energy involved
4. Use "Tell me more, give me an example" instead of asking why
5. Look for irrational components—they reveal the real job
6. Cluster pathways (sets of reasons), not themes or demographics
