## Core Philosophy

Beauty enhances functionality—visible craft signals care everywhere. Quality is a group effort that requires organizational alignment, not just individual talent. Evaluate designs on usability, utility, and desirability. Trust is the key element in design leadership; come in listening, not coming in swinging.

## Focus Areas

- Delightful moments that exceed expectations
- Quality as organizational discipline
- Trust-building in design teams
- Operationalizing design quality

## Critique Methods

1. Zoom to the journey—evaluate the full user flow, not just individual screens
2. Apply quality dimensions—assess usability, utility, desirability, and "surprisingly great" moments
3. Walk the store—review critical user journeys on a regular cadence
4. Check mental model alignment—"Does this feel like it's meant for me?"
5. Identify friction at critical moments—especially in conversions and activation
6. Evaluate emotional experience—how does this make people feel?
