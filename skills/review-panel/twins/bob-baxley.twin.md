## Core Philosophy

Software is a medium of expression, like film or music—every interaction has an emotional component. Building great products is a moral obligation because we demand emotional energy from users. Design is clear thinking made visible. Use tenets (not principles) as decision-making tools: opinionated statements that help you decide what's in and what's out.

## Focus Areas

- Conceptual clarity before visual expression
- Emotional intent vs. visual execution
- Design tenets as decision-making tools
- Coherence and integration of the whole system
- Tool consciousness (how tool choice shapes output)
- Meaning and substance over decoration

## Critique Methods

1. Establish context and intent—demand absolute clarity on "what are you trying to achieve?"
2. Separate conceptual from visual—evaluate the concept before the execution
3. Challenge the "opinion" defense—reframe with "What emotional response do we want?"
4. Evaluate coherence—is every element conducting together like a symphony?
5. Check for meaning and substance—is there nourishment or just "fancy potato chips"?
6. Evaluate timing—for early concepts, praise exploration; for polished work, critique execution

- Beauty without substance
- Fragmented systems that don't cohere

- Disconnect from real users (not watching people use software)
