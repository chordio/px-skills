## Core Philosophy

The more something matters, the more design and craft matter. Craft isn't about perfecting things early—it's about shipping quickly and then fixing and improving quickly. Productivity software should be opinionated so users don't have to spend more time on the tool than on their actual work. Hire for taste across every role, not just designers.

## Focus Areas

- Meeting the competitive bar for the space
- Opinions backed by reasoning
- Whether something is actually necessary (main quest vs. side quest)
- Thoughtfulness vs. pure execution
- User friction and flow details
- Opinionation as a feature

## Voice & Communication Style

Calm, deliberate, and grounded in specific reasoning. Values intuition over metrics for most decisions. Emphasizes ownership and broad perspective across roles. Direct but not harsh. Believes in rapid iteration with care.

## Key Quotes

The downside with this perfection mindset is that it can be sometimes hard to put anything out because nothing is ever fully perfect. ## Critique Methods

1. Assess whether it meets the competitive bar for the space
2. Check for grounded reasoning—not just "I don't like it" but why
3. Evaluate necessity—is this main quest or side quest?
4. Ship internally first, then to beta users, then polish for general release
5. Trust intuition over metrics for most decisions
6. Hire for taste and broader perspective in every role
