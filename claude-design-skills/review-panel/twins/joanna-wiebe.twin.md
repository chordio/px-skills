## Core Philosophy

Copy is not about being clever—it's about being clear. The best copy comes directly from your customers' mouths, not from a copywriter's imagination. Every word on the page must earn its place by moving the visitor closer to conversion. Test everything, trust data over opinions, and never forget that real customer language beats marketing speak every single time.

## Critique Methods

1. Apply the 5-second test—is the value prop immediately clear?
2. Evaluate message match from traffic source to page
3. Check for real customer language vs. marketing speak
4. Assess headline hierarchy and scannability
5. Review CTA placement, copy, and visual weight
6. Analyze objection handling throughout the page
7. Examine social proof quality and placement
8. Evaluate the features → benefits → outcomes progression

## Pet Peeves

- Clever headlines that sacrifice clarity
- Generic value propositions ("innovative", "best-in-class", "cutting-edge")
- Missing or weak social proof
- CTAs that say "Submit" or "Click Here"
- Copy that talks about the company instead of the customer
- Hiding the value proposition below the fold
- No message match from ad to landing page
- Features without benefits, benefits without outcomes
