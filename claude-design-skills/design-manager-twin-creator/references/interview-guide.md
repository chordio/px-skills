# Interview Guide for Twin Creation

Use this guide when interviewing someone to capture their perspective for a digital twin.

## Interview Approach

- Ask one or two questions at a time
- Let them elaborate naturally
- Follow up on interesting threads
- Capture specific examples, not just abstractions

## Core Identity (Start Here)

**Opening:**
> "I'd like to understand how you approach design reviews so we can create a digital twin that thinks like you. Let's start with the basics."

1. "How would you describe your role and what you're responsible for?"
2. "What's your background—how did you develop your design perspective?"

## Philosophy & Principles

**Transition:**
> "Now I'd like to understand what drives your decisions."

3. "What do you believe most strongly about good design?"
4. "Is there a principle you find yourself repeating to your team?"
5. "What's a common 'best practice' you actually disagree with?"

## Focus & Priorities

**Transition:**
> "When you're reviewing someone's work..."

6. "What's the first thing you look at?"
7. "What are the 2-3 things you consistently pay attention to?"
8. "What issues are dealbreakers vs. things you'll let slide?"

## Pet Peeves & Red Flags

**Transition:**
> "Let's talk about what frustrates you."

9. "What mistakes do you see over and over?"
10. "Is there something that immediately signals a problem to you?"
11. "What's a hill you're willing to die on?"

## Communication Style

**Transition:**
> "I want to capture how you actually sound."

12. "Would you describe yourself as more direct or diplomatic in feedback?"
13. "Do you tend to ask questions or give directives?"
14. "Are there phrases you find yourself using a lot?"

**Try to elicit examples:**
> "Can you give me an example of feedback you recently gave?"

## Contextual Adaptation

**Transition:**
> "Different situations might call for different approaches."

15. "How do you adjust your feedback for early concepts vs. polished work?"
16. "Do you review differently for junior vs. senior team members?"
17. "What about when there's time pressure?"

## Domain-Specific Views

**Transition:**
> "Let's go deeper on specific topics."

For each relevant domain (mobile, accessibility, systems, etc.):
18. "What's your take on [domain]? What do you look for?"
19. "What's a common mistake people make with [domain]?"

## Additional Perspectives (Optional)

If the twin will be used beyond design critiques:

**On Team Dynamics:**
20. "How do you think about team structure and collaboration?"
21. "What makes a design team effective?"

**On Career & Growth:**
22. "What advice do you give to designers about their growth?"
23. "What skills do you think are undervalued?"

**On Process:**
24. "What's your ideal design process look like?"
25. "Where do you see teams waste the most time?"

## Closing

**Wrap up:**
> "A few final questions to make sure we capture you accurately."

26. "If someone read your reviews without your name, how would they know it's you?"
27. "What's something people often get wrong about your perspective?"
28. "Anything else that's core to how you think that we haven't covered?"

## Post-Interview

After the interview:
1. Review notes for direct quotes
2. Identify recurring themes
3. Note energy and emphasis
4. Flag areas that need follow-up
5. Draft the twin profile using the template
6. Share draft with subject for validation
