Your product is drifting off-brand. Buttons that don't match. Spacing that feels... wrong. UX patterns that work, technically, but feel generic. Your design system is becoming a suggestion, not a standard.

## 3. The 60% Problem

**Header:** AI design quality has a ceiling. Most teams hit it without realizing.

**Body:**

Here's what the gap actually looks like:

**60% (Where most teams are):**
- AI generates plausible UI that technically works
- Developers accept suggestions because they look reasonable
- Design drift accumulates silently across hundreds of small decisions
- Nobody notices until the product feels "off"

**99% (Where you need to be):**
- AI generates UI that matches your brand standards exactly

- UX decisions reflect your product's established conventions
- Designers trust the output enough to own it

The difference? Context that's deeply integrated, continuously validated, and systematically maintained.

**Callout:** "68% of users abandon products that appear inconsistent or difficult to navigate." That's the cost of the gap.

**Why it matters:** Generic AI output happens when there's no spec. This skill ensures every feature gets the same thoughtful design consideration a senior designer would provide—without the bottleneck.

**Why it matters:** Design managers become bottlenecks not because they're slow, but because they're finite. This skill multiplies their judgment without multiplying their hours.

**Why it matters:** No team has every type of design expert on staff. This skill gives you access to perspectives you'd otherwise never get—for every project, not just the important ones.

## 6. But Can't I Just Prompt Better?

**Header:** The skills look simple. That's the point.

**Body:**

You could write these prompts yourself. The initial results would be impressive. You might even think: "Why would I pay for something I can build in an afternoon?"

Here's what you'd discover over the next six months:

**Month 1:** Your prompt works great for common cases.

**Month 2:** Edge cases start appearing. Prompts get longer, more complex. Different team members create different versions.

**Month 3:** A model update changes behavior. Things that worked now don't. You're debugging prompts instead of shipping features.

**Month 4:** Someone asks "why does the AI keep suggesting that pattern?" Nobody remembers who wrote that part of the prompt.

**Month 5:** You've got prompt debt. Multiple versions floating around. Inconsistent results across the team.

**Month 6:** You realize maintaining prompt quality is a job, not a task. But you don't have headcount for it.

**We've seen this pattern dozens of times.** The 60% → 99% gap isn't about writing better prompts. It's about building systems that maintain quality over time, across model updates, across team changes, across thousands of edge cases.

That's what Chordio does. We maintain the skills so they keep working—and keep improving—without becoming your problem.
